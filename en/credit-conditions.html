General Credit Conditions

Everything you need to know about the operation, rights and obligations related to a Solfianza consumer credit agreement under French law.

1. Credit granting terms

The granting of consumer credit by CA Consumer Finance (Solfianza) is subject to a creditworthiness assessment of the borrower. This assessment takes into account income, recurring charges, banking situation and the FICP consultation. Any credit offer is subject to a 14 calendar day right of withdrawal.

2. Contract formation

The credit agreement is formed by the borrower's acceptance of the personalised offer sent by Solfianza. Acceptance may take place by qualified electronic signature. The contract takes effect the day after the end of the withdrawal period, unless the borrower expressly waives this right for imminent financing needs.

3. Interest rate and APR

The fixed nominal interest rate and the corresponding APR are specified in the pre-contractual information sheet and the contract. The APR includes all mandatory costs (interest, fees, insurance if required). It is calculated in accordance with the method specified in Annex I of Directive 2008/48/EC, transposed into the French Consumer Code.

4. Repayment

Repayment is made by monthly instalments of a fixed amount, on a date agreed upon signing. In the event of a debit failure, a fee of €8 per returned direct debit may be charged. After 30 days of late payment, the entire capital may be made immediately payable (acceleration clause), in addition to late interest at the legal rate increased by 8 points.

5. Early repayment

In accordance with Article L312-34 of the French Consumer Code, early repayment is permitted at any time. For annual repayments of up to €10,000, no indemnity is due. Beyond this, an indemnity of 0.5% (if remaining term > 12 months) or 1% (if remaining term ≤ 12 months) of the capital repaid early applies.

6. Borrower insurance

Borrower insurance is offered as an option. You may take out an alternative insurance policy equivalent to the one offered by Solfianza (substitution right). In the event of activation of guarantees (death, TILA, incapacity), the insurer settles the monthly payments or the remaining capital directly with Solfianza.

7. Modification and transfer

No unilateral modification of the credit conditions is permitted after signing, except in cases provided for by law. The contract may be transferred to another credit institution with prior notification to the borrower.

8. Applicable law and disputes

This credit agreement is governed by French law, in particular the provisions of the French Consumer Code relating to consumer credit (Articles L312-1 and following). In the event of a dispute, we invite you to contact our complaints department first, then if necessary a mediator (CNPM) before any legal proceedings.
